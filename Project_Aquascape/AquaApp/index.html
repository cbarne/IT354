As plants grow, fish thrive; algae grows, fish die. It is a cycle of nature.

pH

The pH is the test of acidity of water. The neutral zone is a 7.0 for alkalinity. While being less then 7.0 is acidic.

pH High

Higher ranges are generally used for marine fish & invertebrates. A decent zone is between 8.0 - 8.4 in most cases.

Ammonia

Ammonia is the waste released by the fish through their gills,uneaten food, & excrement. It can cause a quick imbalance in a tank. Killing fish & plants alike.

Nitrite

It is produced from the biological filter in the aquarium. It converts ammonia into nitrites into nitrates. By itself it is toxic, & in trace amounts can stress a fish / plant to death.

Nitrate

It also is produced from the biological filter in the aquarium.It is converted from nitrites into nitrates. It is the result of waste and uneaten food buildup. To much can cause disease & algae bloom.

Balance

Just like everything in nature. We exist in harmony. Creating a balance is key in an aquatic space so multiple species can thrive.
